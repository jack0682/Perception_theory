# Question: Q-0001 — What is Cohesion?

## Statement

What is the primitive concept of "cohesion" in this theory? Is it a property of objects, a relationship between entities, a field quantity, or something else?

---

## Context

This is the foundational ontological question. The answer determines everything else in the theory: whether we start from objects or fields, whether cohesion is derivative or primitive.

---

## Related Work

- Canonical Spec v1.2 §2 (Foundational Orientation) — Comprehensive answer
- A-0001 (Soft Cohesion Field is primitive)

---

## Proposed Approaches

1. **Approach A: Object-first** — Cohesion is a property of pre-given objects
   - Pros: Familiar, matches intuition
   - Cons: Inherits pre-objective individuation problem
   
2. **Approach B: Field-first** — Cohesion is a graded field u_t : X_t → [0,1]
   - Pros: Avoids circular presupposition; captures pre-objective states
   - Cons: Requires continuous/graded ontology; unfamiliar formally

3. **Approach C: Relational** — Cohesion is a structural relationship (not a property)
   - Pros: Emphasizes relationality; avoids property essentialism
   - Cons: Harder to formalize; still needs mathematical substrate

---

## Resolved Answer

**Approach B (Field-first) is canonical.**

Cohesion is formalized as a graded soft field u_t : X_t → [0,1] that assigns to each relational locus a degree of participatory intensity in formation. This is the primitive entity. Objects are a distinguished region of the formation space (where u is high, stable, distinct) — not the starting point.

**Why this matters:**
- Allows pre-objective formations to be formally represented
- Captures intermediate coherence states
- Makes individuation process explicit, not presupposed
- Enables graded persistence and temporal evolution
