## OUTPUT F: Best Honest Narrative

**What the theory is strongest at:**
SCC introduces a genuinely novel variational structure for formation on graphs: a self-referential energy where two of four terms involve operators that depend on the field being minimized. The static single-formation core is mathematically solid — existence, uniqueness of closure fixed points, a computable phase transition criterion, gradient flow convergence, and Γ-convergence are all rigorously proved. The operator triad (self-completion, self-contrast, self-integration) is a structurally distinctive contribution with no direct analogue in existing phase-field literature.

**What is still open:**
Temporal persistence has a sound proof strategy with three of six gaps closed, but three remain. Multi-formation theory has a well-reasoned architecture but no quantitative results. The diagnostic-energy relationship is one-directional. Parameters are robust but not data-derived.

**What must no longer be claimed:**
- "Formalizes autopoiesis" (fixed operators ≠ self-production)
- "No precedent in OT literature" (mean-field games)
- "First variational Gestalt foundation" (unverified priority)
- "Deeper energy basins" for T7 (Hessian curvature ≠ barrier height)
- Q_morph vanishes on uniform fields (false as stated)

**Best publishable identity:**
*SCC is a self-referential variational theory of formation on graphs that extends Allen-Cahn with two novel energy terms — closure deviation and self-referential distinction — producing formations with provably enhanced stability, a computable phase transition, and a four-component diagnostic vector that decomposes formation quality into binding, separation, morphological articulation, and temporal persistence. The static core is rigorous; the temporal and multi-formation extensions are promising but incomplete.*
