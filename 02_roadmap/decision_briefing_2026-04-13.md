## Executive Brief: The Problem

M3 audit (completed 2026-04-12) surfaced **three critical unresolved problems** in the SCC theory:

### F-1: K=2 Vacuity 🔴 CRITICAL

**What:** K=2 (multi-formation) configurations are only stable if external per-formation masses are fixed  
**Evidence:** exp62, exp63 show K=2 energy E ≈ 4.66; K=1 energy E ≈ 2.25 (K=1 ~50% cheaper)  
**Impact:** All K-field theorems depend on unresolved assumption A-0012 ("fixed K")  
**Status:** No mechanism yet explains why K would be fixed in biological systems

### M-1: K=1 Always Preferred 🔴 CRITICAL

**What:** Energy landscape monotonically prefers K=1 over K>1  
**Evidence:** Direct calculation + empirical confirmation (exp62, exp63, exp71–exp73)  
**Impact:** Cannot explain K>1 emergence from energy minimization alone  
**Status:** Would require new model selection principle (BIC, free energy, etc.) to explain

### MO-1: Morse Theory Inapplicable 🔴 CRITICAL

**What:** Constrained manifold Σ²_M is manifold *with corners* (not smooth)  
**Evidence:** Manifold geometry — smooth Morse theory invalid for manifolds with boundary  
**Impact:** Global optimization analysis incomplete; may need stratified Morse theory  
**Status:** Adds complexity but not fundamentally blocking (can use available results)

## Key Evidence: Why F-1/M-1 Matter

### The Energy Paradox (M-1)

```
K=1 Theory: Single formation with total mass M
  Energy: E_K1(M) ≈ baseline

K=2 Theory: Two formations with masses m₁, m₂ where m₁ + m₂ = M
  Energy: E_K2(m₁, m₂)
  
Finding: E_K2(m₁, m₂) > E_K1(M) for ALL choices of m₁, m₂
         K=2 is always ~50% more expensive energetically

Problem: If energy alone determines K, why would K=2 ever occur?
         Answer: It wouldn't, unless K is fixed externally (F-1)
```

### The Validation Failure

**exp65 (Type A/B validation):**
- Proposed: Two types of K=2 configurations (Type A centered, Type B off-center)
- Tested: 4 different configurations with formation tracking
- Result: **Type B never observed** (0/4 runs)
- Conclusion: Classification hypothesis **RETRACTED**

**What it means:** Our proposed K=2 classification scheme was wrong. But this also reveals: when we try K=2, we always end up in the same attractor (Type A). Why? Likely because K=1 is energetically preferred (M-1).
