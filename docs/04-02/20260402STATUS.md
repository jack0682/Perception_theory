### 6.2. The Answer

**Multi-formation IS possible on a single field** — when formations are sufficiently spatially separated.

**Closure's role: lowers the minimum separation threshold.**
- SCC (a_cl=3.0): K=4 stable from **10×10** grid (d_min ≈ 5)
- Allen-Cahn (a_cl=0): K=4 stable from **15×15** grid (d_min ≈ 7)
- Closure reduces d_min* by **~30%** (T7-Enhanced applied to multi-formation)

**K is kinetically determined:**
- Energy always prefers K=1 (isoperimetric ordering, proved Cat A)
- K>1 formations are **metastable** local minima with exponentially high barriers
- Initial conditions determine K; closure enhances persistence at given K

### 6.3. Stability Hierarchy (Final)

```
NECESSARY for multi-formation:
  1. Phase separation (double-well E_bd) — creates formations
  2. Sufficient spatial separation (d > d_min*) — prevents merge

ENHANCING:
  3. Closure (E_cl) — reduces d_min* by ~30% (T7 for multi-formation)
  4. K-field architecture — guarantees separation by construction

NOT NECESSARY:
  5. Repulsion, simplex barrier, fixed per-formation mass
```

### 6.4. CN14 (Final)

**CN14. Closure Expands Multi-Formation Stability.** K>1 well-separated formations are metastable on a single field. Closure reduces the critical separation distance d_min* by ~30% compared to pure Allen-Cahn, extending multi-formation stability to smaller domains. K=1 is always the global energy minimum (isoperimetric); K>1 is kinetically maintained.
