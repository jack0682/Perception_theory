## 2. Relation to Layered Ambient Architecture

The two trajectories live in the **Layer II shared-pool ambient**

$$
\widetilde{\Sigma}^{K_{\mathrm{field}}}_M(G_t),
\qquad K_{\mathrm{field}} = 4, \;\; M = 90, \;\; G_t = T^2_{20}.
$$

At $t = 0$ both trajectories sit in the same labelled active-set cell

$$
S_A^\varepsilon(G_t), \qquad A = \{1, 2, 3\}, \qquad K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon = 3.
$$

Slot $u^{(4)}$ is the inactive spare ($u^{(4)} \equiv 0$). The K-jump is a transition

$$
\mathbf u(t^{*-}) \in S_3^\varepsilon(G_t), \qquad \mathbf u(t^{*+}) \in S_2^\varepsilon(G_t),
$$

i.e. $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon$ drops from 3 to 2.

If a multi-merger event ($3 \to 1$) occurs instead, it is *recorded and classified separately* (§7); it does not invalidate the run.

**No statement about Whitney structure on $S_A^\varepsilon$ or $S_r^\varepsilon$ is made.** No statement about smoothness of Layer II is made. The K-jump is event-only, with no inheritance assertion.

## 3. Target Claim

### 3.1 NQ-242c-Standard-Incompleteness Target

**Target.** There exist two continuous trajectories

$$
\mathbf u_A(t),\; \mathbf u_B(t) \;\in\; \widetilde{\Sigma}^{K_{\mathrm{field}}}_M(T^2_{20})
$$

generated by the same multi-field projected gradient flow (same $K_{\mathrm{field}}$, $M$, graph $G$, threshold $\varepsilon$, integrator, projection, stopping rule, energy parameters), such that:

1. Both start in the same active-set region $S_A^\varepsilon(G)$ with $|A| = 3$.
2. Both undergo a K-jump from $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon = 3$ to $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon = 2$.
3. Their qualitative pre-jump signatures agree:
   $$
   \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_A(t^{-}_A)) \;=_{\tau}\; \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_B(t^{-}_B)).
   $$
4. Their post-jump signatures differ beyond tolerance:
   $$
   \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_A(t^{+}_A)) \;\ne_{\tau}\; \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_B(t^{+}_B)).
   $$

### 3.2 Conclusion if validated

> $\sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}$ is **not** a complete invariant for K-jump inheritance under the constructed conditions on $T^2_{20}$.

### 3.3 Explicit non-conclusions

- This does **not** prove $\sigma_{\mathrm{rich}}$ sufficiency. σ-rich components are computed for diagnostic purposes only.
- This does **not** generalize to all graphs. The result, if positive, holds for the specific $(G, K_{\mathrm{field}}, M, \varepsilon, \text{energy params})$ configuration only.
- This does **not** resolve OP-0008. OP-0008 retains 🟠 HIGH severity.
- This does **not** resolve OP-0005 (K-Selection).
- This does **not** authorize promotion of any object to canonical.

## 4. Graph and State-Space Setup

### 4.1 Graph

$$
G = T^2_{20}, \qquad X = \mathbb Z_{20} \times \mathbb Z_{20}, \qquad |X| = 400.
$$

Torus distance:

$$
d_T\big((a,b),(c,d)\big)
=
\sqrt{
\min(|a - c|, 20 - |a - c|)^2
+
\min(|b - d|, 20 - |b - d|)^2
}.
$$

Adjacency: 4-regular nearest-neighbor on the torus,

$$
N(x, y) = \mathbf 1\{d_T(x, y) = 1\}.
$$

This matches the existing `GraphState.torus_2d(20, 20)` constructor used throughout the codebase.

### 4.2 Layer II ambient instance

$$
\widetilde{\Sigma}^{4}_{90}(T^2_{20})
=
\Big\{
\mathbf u = (u^{(1)}, u^{(2)}, u^{(3)}, u^{(4)}) \in [0,1]^{4 \times 400}
:\;
\sum_{j=1}^{4} \sum_{x \in X} u^{(j)}(x) = 90,
\;\;
\sum_{j=1}^{4} u^{(j)}(x) \le 1 \;\forall x
\Big\}.
$$

### 4.3 Activity threshold

Default convention from `..._candidate.md` §6.2 and `K_status_commitment.md` §6.1:

$$
\bar m = \frac{M}{K_{\mathrm{field}}} = \frac{90}{4} = 22.5,
\qquad
\varepsilon = 0.01 \cdot \bar m = 0.225.
$$

### 4.4 Active count and K-jump

$$
m_j(\mathbf u) = \sum_{x \in X} u^{(j)}(x),
\qquad
K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u) = \#\{j : m_j(\mathbf u) > \varepsilon\}.
$$

A trajectory time $t^*$ is a K-jump iff

$$
K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u(t^{*-})) > K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u(t^{*+})).
$$

In this protocol the **target jump** is $3 \to 2$. A $3 \to 1$ jump is recorded as a multi-merger event and processed separately (§7 multi-merger handling).

## 5. Initial Configurations

### 5.1 Active-set design

Both configurations have the same active set $A = \{1, 2, 3\}$ and inactive slot $u^{(4)} \equiv 0$.

Equal per-formation mass:

$$
m_1(\mathbf u(0)) = m_2(\mathbf u(0)) = m_3(\mathbf u(0)) = 30,
\qquad
m_4(\mathbf u(0)) = 0,
\qquad
\sum_j m_j = 90 = M.
$$

### 5.2 Bump construction

Per-formation Gaussian bump in torus distance:

$$
\tilde u^{(j)}(x) = \exp\!\left(-\frac{d_T(x, c_j)^2}{2 \sigma_b^2}\right),
\qquad
\sigma_b = 2.0,
$$

then normalize and project to enforce mass and simplex constraints:

1. Normalize: $u^{(j)}(x) = \tilde u^{(j)}(x) \cdot m_j / \sum_x \tilde u^{(j)}(x)$.
2. Clip to $[0, 1]$.
3. If after clip the simplex constraint $\sum_j u^{(j)}(x) \le 1$ is violated at any $x$, scale all $u^{(j)}$ by a uniform factor $\le 1$ to clear the violation, then re-normalize each $u^{(j)}$ to enforce mass via `project_volume` (the existing `scc/optimizer.project_volume` utility).
4. Re-iterate clip + simplex check + mass projection up to 3 times. If the simplex constraint cannot be satisfied with the chosen $\sigma_b$, decrease $\sigma_b$ to $1.5$ and retry; if still infeasible, decrease per-formation mass to $20$ each (and adjust $M$ accordingly), recording the deviation explicitly in the results.

The simplex barrier in the dynamics ($\lambda_{\mathrm{bar}} \cdot \max(0, \sum_j u^{(j)} - 1)^2$) provides a soft enforcement during gradient flow; the initial-state projection above is the discrete-state equivalent.

### 5.3 Configuration A — Equilateral disk-triangle

Centers (integer torus coordinates, approximately equilateral with side length 10):

$$
c_1^A = (5, 5), \qquad c_2^A = (15, 5), \qquad c_3^A = (10, 14).
$$

Pairwise torus distances:

$$
d_T(c_1^A, c_2^A) = 10, \qquad d_T(c_1^A, c_3^A) = \sqrt{25 + 81} = \sqrt{106} \approx 10.30,
\qquad d_T(c_2^A, c_3^A) = \sqrt{25 + 81} \approx 10.30.
$$

This is the closest integer-grid approximation of an equilateral triangle of side $\approx 10$ on the torus. The pair $\{c_1, c_2\}$ is the *minimum-distance* pair under the integer-grid approximation; full $D_3$ symmetry is broken weakly. Symmetry-breaking noise from the optimizer determines which pair merges first; this is part of the test (Configuration A may or may not produce a clean K-jump, and the result must be reported either way).

### 5.4 Configuration B — Isosceles / compressed disk-triangle

Centers (compressed vertically):

$$
c_1^B = (5, 5), \qquad c_2^B = (15, 5), \qquad c_3^B = (10, 11).
$$

Pairwise torus distances:

$$
d_T(c_1^B, c_2^B) = 10, \qquad d_T(c_1^B, c_3^B) = \sqrt{25 + 36} = \sqrt{61} \approx 7.81,
\qquad d_T(c_2^B, c_3^B) = \sqrt{25 + 36} \approx 7.81.
$$

The minimum-distance pairs are $\{c_1, c_3\}$ and $\{c_2, c_3\}$, equivalent under $\mathbb Z_2$ horizontal reflection. Symmetry-breaking noise selects one of them.

### 5.5 Why these configurations should share σ-standard at $t = 0$

Both A and B use **identical per-formation Gaussian bump profiles** (same $\sigma_b$, same mass). The per-formation Hessian spectrum is therefore identical between A and B at $t = 0$ to within graph-translation differences. The σ-standard implementation in `scc/sigma_rich.py::_sigma_standard` produces a multiplicity-tagged eigenvalue cluster tuple from the FD-Hessian of $\bar u = \frac{1}{K} \sum_j u^{(j)}$, which is also approximately equal between A and B at $t = 0$ (the aggregate mass distributions differ in centroid arrangement but not in per-bump shape).

Eigenvalue tolerance $\tau_\lambda = 10^{-3}$ (default; configurable). Qualitative equality means same cluster sizes, same multiplicity tags, same eigenvalues to within $\tau_\lambda$.

## 6. Dynamics

### 6.1 Energy

The Layer II energy used is the existing `scc/multi.py` form:

$$
\mathcal E_K(\mathbf u)
\;=\;
\sum_{j=1}^{K_{\mathrm{field}}} \mathcal E_{\mathrm{self}}(u^{(j)})
\;+\;
\lambda_{\mathrm{rep}} \sum_{j < k} \langle u^{(j)}, u^{(k)} \rangle
\;+\;
\lambda_{\mathrm{bar}} \sum_x \max\!\big(0,\; \textstyle\sum_j u^{(j)}(x) - 1\big)^2,
$$

where $\mathcal E_{\mathrm{self}}$ is the canonical SCC 4-term energy (closure + separation + boundary + transport-stub) per `scc/energy.py`, $\langle u^{(j)}, u^{(k)} \rangle = \sum_x u^{(j)}(x) u^{(k)}(x)$, and the simplex barrier is the soft penalty enforcing $\sum_j u^{(j)}(x) \le 1$.

**Default parameters** (configurable via the script):

| Symbol | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|
| $K_{\mathrm{field}}$ | 4 | architectural cap |
| $M$ | 90 | total mass |
| $m_j(0)$ | 30 (j∈A), 0 (j∉A) | per-formation initial mass |
| $\sigma_b$ | 2.0 | initial bump width |
| $\lambda_{\mathrm{rep}}$ | 10.0 | inter-formation repulsion (multi.py default) |
| $\lambda_{\mathrm{bar}}$ | 100.0 | simplex barrier (multi.py default) |
| $\beta_{\mathrm{bd}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{bd}}, w_{\mathrm{cl}}, \ldots$ | repo `ParameterRegistry` defaults | SCC 4-term parameters |
| $\varepsilon$ | 0.225 | activity threshold |

### 6.2 Numerical integration

Discrete projected gradient descent on each formation in turn (the existing `scc/multi.py::_optimize_k_fields` routine):

```
for τ = 1, ..., max_iter:
    for k = 1, ..., K_field:
        g_intra = ec.gradient(u^(k))
        g_rep   = λ_rep · Σ_{j≠k} u^(j)
        g_bar   = 2 λ_bar · max(0, S − 1)         where S = Σ_j u^(j)
        g_total = g_intra + g_rep + g_bar
        g_proj  = g_total − mean(g_total)         // project onto T(Σ_m)
        u^(k)   ← project_volume(u^(k) − dt · g_proj, m_k(0))
```

Note: this integrator preserves *initial mass* per formation slot, but `project_volume` is the simplex projection of `scc/optimizer.py` which sets negative entries to zero before re-normalizing. This means $m_j$ can effectively decay (mass migrates within the slot via clipping) but the slot-level constraint $\sum_x u^{(j)}(x) = m_j(0)$ is re-enforced each step. To allow K-jump (mass migration *between* formations), the protocol must use a *variable-per-formation-mass* variant.

### 6.3 Variable-per-formation-mass variant (required for K-jumps)

The default `_optimize_k_fields` enforces fixed per-formation mass via `project_volume`. For NQ-242c, we need *total* mass conservation only, not per-formation. The protocol requires one of two modifications:

**Option D-1 (recommended).** Replace per-slot `project_volume(u^(k), m_k(0))` with a joint projection onto the total-mass simplex

$$
\Pi_{\widetilde{\Sigma}^{K}_M}\;:\; (u^{(1)}, \ldots, u^{(K)}) \mapsto \text{argmin}_{\mathbf v \in \widetilde{\Sigma}^K_M} \|\mathbf v - \mathbf u\|_2^2,
$$

solved by projecting onto $\sum_{j,x} u^{(j)}(x) = M$ then clipping to $[0, 1]$ and re-projecting (Dykstra-style) until simplex constraint holds.

**Option D-2 (simpler).** Keep `project_volume` per formation but allow $m_j$ itself to be a degree of freedom: project onto $\{u^{(j)} \in [0,1]^X\}$ and recompute $m_j = \sum_x u^{(j)}(x)$ each step; then rescale all $m_j$ by $M / \sum_j m_j$ to enforce total-mass conservation.

The script (§14) implements **Option D-2** as the simpler variant. Option D-1 is preferred theoretically and is the documented upgrade path.

### 6.4 Time step, max iterations, stopping

| Symbol | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|
| $dt$ | 1e-3 | initial time step (adaptive per `_optimize_k_fields`) |
| `max_iter` | 5000 | maximum outer iterations |
| stopping criterion | $\max_k \|g_k\|_\infty < 10^{-5}$ for ≥ 50 consecutive iterations | gradient stagnation |
| seed | 42 (default; configurable) | NumPy `RandomState` |

### 6.5 Snapshot recording

Snapshots $\mathbf u(\tau)$ are recorded every `snapshot_every = 25` steps. From snapshots we compute:

- $m_j(\tau)$ for $j = 1, \ldots, K_{\mathrm{field}}$;
- $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u(\tau))$;
- $\sigma_{\mathrm{rich}}(\mathbf u(\tau))$ (full namedtuple) at the K-jump-bracketing snapshots (pre-jump and post-stabilization).

Recording $\sigma_{\mathrm{rich}}$ at every snapshot is expensive (Hessian FD is $O(n^2)$ in `n = 400` per snapshot $\approx$ 1 minute). The protocol records it only at snapshots flagged for K-jump bracketing.

### 6.6 K-jump detection

A K-jump time $\tau^*$ is detected when

$$
K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u(\tau^*_-)) > K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u(\tau^*))
$$

at consecutive recorded snapshots. The first such $\tau^*$ is the *first K-jump time*. Subsequent K-jumps in the same trajectory are recorded but the protocol focuses on the first.

**Multi-merger flag.** If $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon$ drops by more than 1 between two consecutive snapshots, the multi-merger flag is set and the trajectory is classified as `multi_merger=True`. This does not abort the run; both A and B can be multi-merger and their σ-standard pre/post can still be compared.

**No K-jump flag.** If at the end of `max_iter` no K-jump has occurred, `no_k_jump=True` is recorded and the run is reported as F2 failure mode (§9.4).

## 7. Standard Signature Extraction

### 7.1 Definition used by this protocol

The σ-standard used here is the existing `scc/sigma_rich.py::_sigma_standard(eigvals)` operating on the FD-Hessian eigenvalues of the aggregate field

$$
\bar u(\mathbf u) := \tfrac{1}{K_{\mathrm{field}}} \sum_{j=1}^{K_{\mathrm{field}}} u^{(j)},
$$

evaluated at a snapshot $\mathbf u(\tau)$. The Hessian is built by `_build_hessian(u_mean, graph_state, params)` via symmetric finite differences with $h = 10^{-5}$.

The output is a tuple of clusters

$$
\sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u) = \big( (n_1, \mathrm{mult}\text{-}n_1, \lambda_1), (n_2, \mathrm{mult}\text{-}n_2, \lambda_2), \ldots \big),
$$

where $\lambda_1 < \lambda_2 < \cdots$ are the cluster representatives and $n_k$ is the cluster size.

**Aliasing.** In the working notation of `sigma_multi_trajectory.md`, the same object is sometimes called $\sigma^A$. For this protocol:

$$
\sigma_{\mathrm{standard}} \;:=\; \sigma^A \;:=\; \text{output of } \mathtt{compute\_sigma\_rich(\mathbf u, \ldots).sigma\_standard}.
$$

### 7.2 Scope and limitations of this σ-standard

The implementation builds the Hessian on the *aggregate* field $\bar u$, not on the *full* multi-formation state $\mathbf u$. This is a working-only Cat B sketch placeholder per `scc/sigma_rich.py` docstring. The full multi-formation joint Hessian on $K_{\mathrm{field}} \cdot |X|$ degrees of freedom is not implemented in the current code base.

**Consequence for WQ-1:** the σ-standard test in this protocol is a *projected* σ-standard test, not the full multi-formation σ^A test. If C1 / C2 outcomes are positive on the projected σ-standard, the conclusion is restricted to the projected σ-standard; full-σ^A claims require future joint-Hessian implementation. This is an explicit limitation, recorded in the results.

### 7.3 Tolerance

$$
\tau_\lambda = 10^{-3}, \qquad \tau_{\mathrm{cluster}} = 10^{-3}.
$$

Two σ-standard tuples agree iff:

1. They have the same number of clusters.
2. For each cluster index $k$, the cluster sizes $n_k^A, n_k^B$ are equal.
3. For each cluster index $k$, $|\lambda_k^A - \lambda_k^B| < \tau_\lambda$.
4. The multiplicity tags $\mathrm{mult}\text{-}n_k$ are identical (this is automatic given (2)).

Mismatch is recorded with the offending cluster index and $|\lambda_k^A - \lambda_k^B|$ value.

### 7.4 Pre-jump and post-jump sampling times

For each trajectory $X \in \{A, B\}$, let $\tau^*_X$ be the first K-jump time.

- **Pre-jump sample**: $t^{-}_X := \tau^*_X - 25$ (one snapshot before the jump). If $\tau^*_X < 25$, use $t^{-}_X = 0$.
- **Post-jump sample**: $t^{+}_X := \tau^*_X + n_{\mathrm{stab}} \cdot 25$ where $n_{\mathrm{stab}} = 4$ snapshots (post-stabilization).

If $t^{+}_X$ exceeds `max_iter`, use the last available snapshot.

## 8. Rich Signature Diagnostics

The full σ-rich namedtuple is recorded at $t^{-}_X$ and $t^{+}_X$ for both trajectories.

### 8.1 Components

Per `scc/sigma_rich.py::compute_sigma_rich`:

- `sigma_standard`: tuple of $(n_k, \text{mult-tag}, \lambda_k)$ — already used for C1/C2.
- `centroids`: $(K, d) = (4, 2)$ array, per-formation $u$-weighted centroids on the torus.
- `orientations`: $(K, d, d) = (4, 2, 2)$ array, per-formation inertia tensors.
- `wigner_data`: $(K, K, 2, 2) = (4, 4, 2, 2)$ array, pairwise Goldstone-pair 2x2 effective Hamiltonians.

### 8.2 Torus-coordinate caution

The `centroids` computation in `compute_centroids` is a flat Cartesian moment $c_j = \sum_x u^{(j)}(x) \cdot x / m_j$. On the torus, this is *not* the geodesic centroid for formations near the seam. The protocol records both the flat centroid (default) and a torus-aware centroid (Frechet mean on torus, `_torus_centroid`) — the script computes both and reports both for completeness; the canonical comparison uses the flat centroid (matching the existing `compute_sigma_rich` output).

### 8.3 Status of σ-rich diagnostics

- σ-rich components are **diagnostic only** in this protocol.
- σ-rich determinism is **not tested** by C1–C4.
- σ-rich values are recorded so that downstream WQ-3 (σ-rich minimal packet analysis) can use them.
- No claim about σ-rich sufficiency is made.

## 9. Pass / Fail Criteria

### 9.1 C1 — Same pre-jump σ-standard

$$
\sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_A(t^{-}_A)) \;=_{\tau}\; \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_B(t^{-}_B)).
$$

**Status:** PASS iff the agreement test of §7.3 returns True.

### 9.2 C2 — Different post-jump σ-standard

$$
\sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_A(t^{+}_A)) \;\ne_{\tau}\; \sigma_{\mathrm{standard}}(\mathbf u_B(t^{+}_B)).
$$

**Status:** PASS iff the agreement test of §7.3 returns False *and* the offending cluster index has $|\lambda_k^A - \lambda_k^B| > 10 \tau_\lambda$ (10× tolerance margin to rule out marginal numerical noise).

### 9.3 C3 — Same protocol

Both runs use:

- same graph $G = T^2_{20}$;
- same $K_{\mathrm{field}} = 4$, $M = 90$;
- same $\varepsilon = 0.225$;
- same energy parameters (`ParameterRegistry`);
- same $\lambda_{\mathrm{rep}}$, $\lambda_{\mathrm{bar}}$;
- same integrator (`_optimize_k_fields` Option D-2 variant);
- same `dt`, `max_iter`, stopping rule, snapshot interval;
- same seed.

**Status:** PASS iff all eight items match exactly between A and B. The script enforces this by sharing a single parameter dict.

### 9.4 C4 — Robustness

Repeat the construction under at least one of:

- **Center perturbation:** Configuration B center $c_3^B$ moved by $\Delta = (0, \pm 1)$.
- **Bump width perturbation:** $\sigma_b \in \{1.5, 2.5\}$.
- **Time step perturbation:** $dt \in \{5 \times 10^{-4}, 2 \times 10^{-3}\}$.
- **Seed perturbation:** at least one alternative seed; report whether C1 ∧ C2 still hold.
- **Tolerance sweep:** $\tau_\lambda \in \{10^{-2}, 10^{-3}, 10^{-4}\}$.

**Status:** PASS iff at least one robustness check is run and C1 ∧ C2 hold under it.

### 9.5 Outcome classification

| Outcome | Criteria met | Reported as |
|---|---|---|
| **success** | C1 ∧ C2 ∧ C3 ∧ C4 | NQ-242c supports σ-standard incompleteness for the projected σ-standard on $T^2_{20}$ |
| **weak_success** | C1 ∧ C2 ∧ C3, C4 not run or fails | Candidate evidence; robustness inconclusive |
| **failed** | C1 fails OR C2 fails OR C3 fails | Protocol did not falsify σ-standard incompleteness; classify per failure modes §9.6 |

### 9.6 Failure modes

- **F1 — pre-jump signatures unequal.** Configurations A and B do not agree at $t^{-}$ at the chosen tolerance. The construction does not test σ-standard incompleteness; it tests something else. *Adjust:* lower $\tau_\lambda$, redesign B to share more of A's per-bump spectrum, or accept that the projected σ-standard cannot be made to coincide on these configurations.
- **F2 — no K-jump.** Neither trajectory undergoes a K-jump within `max_iter`. *Adjust:* increase $\lambda_{\mathrm{rep}}$, decrease initial separation, increase `max_iter`, or reduce per-formation mass.
- **F3 — post-jump signatures equal.** Both trajectories produce the same projected σ-standard at $t^{+}$. The construction does not falsify σ-standard inheritance. *Adjust:* increase geometric difference between A and B, run on a less-symmetric base graph (e.g. rectangular torus $T^2_{20\times30}$ per `nq242c_explicit_construction.md` §3.4 mitigation), or consider that the projected σ-standard is too coarse and the full joint Hessian σ-standard is the correct test.
- **F4 — hidden numerical separation.** σ-standard agrees pre-jump and post-jump within tolerance, but other diagnostics (centroids, orientations, Wigner data, raw eigenvalue magnitudes below $\tau_\lambda$) clearly distinguish A and B. *Report:* the construction *suggests* that σ-standard at the chosen tolerance is insensitive but other diagnostics see the difference. This is a partial-incompleteness observation, not a clean F1/F2/F3.

Each failure mode is a *valid scientific outcome*; the protocol does not retry until success. A single primary run + at least one robustness check are reported regardless of outcome.
