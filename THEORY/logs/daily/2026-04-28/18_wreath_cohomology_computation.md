## §1. The Extension

The wreath product $D_4 \wr S_2$ fits into the short exact sequence:
$$1 \to D_4 \times D_4 \to D_4 \wr S_2 \to S_2 \to 1, \tag{1.1}$$
where $D_4 \times D_4$ is the kernel (base subgroup) and $S_2 = \mathbb{Z}/2$ is the quotient (swap).

$|D_4 \wr S_2| = 8^2 \cdot 2 = 128$.

Group structure: elements are $(g_1, g_2; \sigma)$ with $g_1, g_2 \in D_4$, $\sigma \in \{e, \tau\}$. Multiplication:
$(g_1, g_2; e) \cdot (h_1, h_2; e) = (g_1 h_1, g_2 h_2; e)$
$(g_1, g_2; e) \cdot (h_1, h_2; \tau) = (g_1 h_1, g_2 h_2; \tau)$
$(g_1, g_2; \tau) \cdot (h_1, h_2; e) = (g_1 h_2, g_2 h_1; \tau)$
$(g_1, g_2; \tau) \cdot (h_1, h_2; \tau) = (g_1 h_2, g_2 h_1; e)$

## §2. Abelianization of $D_4 \wr S_2$

$H^1(BG; \mathbb{Z}/2) = \mathrm{Hom}(G, \mathbb{Z}/2) = (G^{\mathrm{ab}})^* \otimes \mathbb{Z}/2$, where $G^{\mathrm{ab}} = G / [G, G]$.

So we need to compute $(D_4 \wr S_2)^{\mathrm{ab}}$ tensored with $\mathbb{Z}/2$.

### 2.1 Abelianization of $D_4$

$D_4 = \langle r, s : r^4 = s^2 = e, srs = r^{-1} \rangle$.

Commutator: $[r, s] = r s r^{-1} s^{-1} = r \cdot r \cdot s \cdot s^{-1} = r^2$ (using $srs = r^{-1}$, so $s r^{-1} s = r$, hence $r s r^{-1} s^{-1} = r \cdot s r^{-1} s \cdot s^{-1} \cdot s^{-1} = r \cdot r \cdot s^{-2} = r^2$). So $[D_4, D_4] = \langle r^2 \rangle = \{e, r^2\} \cong \mathbb{Z}/2$.

$D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} = D_4 / \langle r^2 \rangle$. Generators: $\bar r = r$ and $\bar s = s$, with $\bar r^2 = e$ (since $r^2 \in $ kernel) and $\bar s^2 = e$. Plus $\bar s \bar r = \bar r \bar s$ (abelian). So $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} \cong (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2$, generated by $\bar r$ and $\bar s$.

In characters: irreps of $D_4$ that factor through abelianization = 1-dim irreps: $A_1, A_2, B_1, B_2$ (4 total). $E$ is 2-dim, doesn't factor.

### 2.2 Abelianization of $D_4 \wr S_2$

Commutator subgroup of $D_4 \wr S_2$:
- Includes $[D_4 \times D_4, D_4 \times D_4] = [D_4, D_4] \times [D_4, D_4] = \mathbb{Z}/2 \times \mathbb{Z}/2$.
- Plus commutators involving $\tau$: $[\tau, (g_1, g_2)] = \tau (g_1, g_2) \tau^{-1} (g_1, g_2)^{-1} = (g_2, g_1) (g_1^{-1}, g_2^{-1}) = (g_2 g_1^{-1}, g_1 g_2^{-1})$. For $g_1 \neq g_2$: nontrivial commutator. Specifically, takes value $(g_2 g_1^{-1}, g_1 g_2^{-1})$.

So $[D_4 \wr S_2, D_4 \wr S_2]$ includes elements $(h, h^{-1})$ for $h \in D_4$, in addition to $\langle r^2 \rangle \times \langle r^2 \rangle$.

The image of $(D_4 \times D_4)$ in the abelianization is $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} \times D_4^{\mathrm{ab}}$ modulo $\{(h, h^{-1}): h \in D_4^{\mathrm{ab}}\}$. Since $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}}$ is abelian (Klein 4), $h^{-1} = h$, so $(h, h^{-1}) = (h, h)$. Modding out by $\{(h, h)\}$ gives $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} \times D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} / \mathrm{diag}$, which equals $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}}$ via the antidiagonal map $(g_1, g_2) \mapsto g_1 g_2^{-1}$. So this part contributes $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}} = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2$.

But wait, that gives only one copy of $D_4^{\mathrm{ab}}$. Combined with $S_2$, we get $(\mathbb{Z}/2)^2 \times \mathbb{Z}/2 = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$.

Let me verify carefully.

### 2.3 Direct computation via generators and relations

Generators of $D_4 \wr S_2$: $r_1 = (r, e; e)$, $s_1 = (s, e; e)$, $r_2 = (e, r; e)$, $s_2 = (e, s; e)$, $\tau = (e, e; \tau)$.

In the abelianization, all commutators are killed:
- $[r_1, s_1]$ kills $r_1^2$.
- $[r_2, s_2]$ kills $r_2^2$.
- $[\tau, r_1] = \tau r_1 \tau^{-1} r_1^{-1} = r_2 r_1^{-1}$. So $r_2 = r_1$ in $G^{\mathrm{ab}}$.
- $[\tau, r_2] = r_1 r_2^{-1}$, gives $r_1 = r_2$ (same as above).
- $[\tau, s_1] = s_2 s_1^{-1}$, so $s_2 = s_1$ in $G^{\mathrm{ab}}$.

After identifications:
- $r_1 = r_2 =: \bar r$ with $\bar r^2 = e$.
- $s_1 = s_2 =: \bar s$ with $\bar s^2 = e$.
- $\tau$ with $\tau^2 = e$.
- All commute (abelian).

So $(D_4 \wr S_2)^{\mathrm{ab}} = \langle \bar r, \bar s, \tau : \bar r^2 = \bar s^2 = \tau^2 = e, \text{all commute} \rangle = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$.

**Confirmed**: $(D_4 \wr S_2)^{\mathrm{ab}} = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$.

### 2.4 $H^1(B(D_4 \wr S_2); \mathbb{Z}/2)$

For finite group $G$:
$$H^1(BG; \mathbb{Z}/2) = \mathrm{Hom}(G^{\mathrm{ab}}, \mathbb{Z}/2) = \mathrm{Hom}((\mathbb{Z}/2)^3, \mathbb{Z}/2) = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3.$$

**Confirmed**: $H^1 = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$. ✓

### 2.5 Three explicit generators

The three generators of $H^1$:
- **$\chi_r$**: linear character $D_4 \wr S_2 \to \mathbb{Z}/2$ that sends $r_1, r_2 \mapsto 1$ and $s_1, s_2, \tau \mapsto 0$. Detects rotation parity.
- **$\chi_s$**: sends $s_1, s_2 \mapsto 1$ and $r_1, r_2, \tau \mapsto 0$. Detects reflection parity.
- **$\chi_\tau$**: sends $\tau \mapsto 1$ and $r_1, r_2, s_1, s_2 \mapsto 0$. Detects swap parity.

These are the three independent linear characters. They span $\mathrm{Hom}(G, \mathbb{Z}/2) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$.

## §3. Verification by Lyndon-Hochschild-Serre Spectral Sequence

### 3.1 Setup

For the short exact sequence (1.1):
$$1 \to N := D_4 \times D_4 \to G := D_4 \wr S_2 \to Q := S_2 \to 1,$$
the LHS spectral sequence converges:
$$E_2^{p,q} = H^p(Q; H^q(N; \mathbb{Z}/2)) \Rightarrow H^{p+q}(G; \mathbb{Z}/2).$$

For $H^1(G; \mathbb{Z}/2)$ ($p + q = 1$): $E_2^{0, 1} \oplus E_2^{1, 0}$ (modulo extension).

### 3.2 $E_2^{1, 0}$: $H^1(Q; H^0(N; \mathbb{Z}/2))$

$H^0(N; \mathbb{Z}/2) = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^N = \mathbb{Z}/2$ (constants).

$H^1(Q; \mathbb{Z}/2) = H^1(S_2; \mathbb{Z}/2) = \mathbb{Z}/2$.

$E_2^{1, 0} = \mathbb{Z}/2$. **One generator: $\chi_\tau$**.

### 3.3 $E_2^{0, 1}$: $(H^1(N; \mathbb{Z}/2))^Q$

$H^1(N; \mathbb{Z}/2) = H^1(D_4 \times D_4; \mathbb{Z}/2)$. By Künneth:
$$H^1(D_4 \times D_4; \mathbb{Z}/2) = H^1(D_4; \mathbb{Z}/2) \oplus H^1(D_4; \mathbb{Z}/2) = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2 \oplus (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2 = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^4.$$

(Using $H^1(D_4; \mathbb{Z}/2) = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2$ from §2.1.)

Generators: $\chi_{r_1}, \chi_{s_1}, \chi_{r_2}, \chi_{s_2}$.

$Q = S_2$ acts on $N = D_4 \times D_4$ by swap. On characters:
- $\tau \cdot \chi_{r_1} = \chi_{r_2}$ (and vice versa).
- $\tau \cdot \chi_{s_1} = \chi_{s_2}$ (and vice versa).

$Q$-invariants: $(H^1(N))^Q = $ characters fixed by swap.
- $\chi_{r_1} + \chi_{r_2}$: fixed (both swap to each other, sum invariant). ✓
- $\chi_{s_1} + \chi_{s_2}$: fixed. ✓
- $\chi_{r_1} - \chi_{r_2}$: swapped to its negative. NOT fixed.
- $\chi_{s_1} - \chi_{s_2}$: NOT fixed.

So $E_2^{0, 1} = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2$, generated by $\chi_{r_1} + \chi_{r_2}$ and $\chi_{s_1} + \chi_{s_2}$.

These correspond to the **diagonal** characters: $\chi_r$ (sends both $r_1, r_2 \to 1$) and $\chi_s$ (sends both $s_1, s_2 \to 1$).

### 3.4 Differential $d_2: E_2^{0, 1} \to E_2^{2, 0}$

$E_2^{2, 0} = H^2(Q; \mathbb{Z}/2) = H^2(S_2; \mathbb{Z}/2) = \mathbb{Z}/2$ (Steenrod-square generator).

The $d_2$ differential sends a $Q$-invariant $H^1(N)$ class to the obstruction to extending it to a $G$-character. For our wreath product: this differential is **zero** because the wreath structure splits (semidirect product with split section).

So $E_3 = E_2$, and convergence: $H^1(G; \mathbb{Z}/2) = E_\infty^{1, 0} \oplus E_\infty^{0, 1} = \mathbb{Z}/2 \oplus (\mathbb{Z}/2)^2 = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$.

**Confirmed via spectral sequence**: $(\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$. ✓

## §6. σ_multi^(D) Implications

With $H^1 = (\mathbb{Z}/2)^3$ confirmed, σ_multi^(D)'s lowest-cohomology fingerprint is:
- Three binary labels per K=2 configuration.
- Distinguishes:
  - "Rotation parity matched between formations" vs not.
  - "Reflection parity matched" vs not.
  - "Swap symmetric vs anti-symmetric" (whether the orbit type has the pair-swap involution).

For typical K=2 with $D_4$-symmetric disks: all three labels are 0 (trivial in cohomology) since the configuration is fully $G$-invariant. So σ_multi^(D) for these specific configurations is **trivial cohomology** in $H^1$.

To get non-trivial $H^1$ classes, the configuration needs reduced symmetry (e.g., disks with $\mathbb{Z}_2 \subset D_4$ stabilizer instead of full $D_4$).

**Phase 4 finding**: $H^1$ is **less informative** for symmetric configurations than expected. To distinguish symmetric configs, need higher cohomology ($H^2$+).

This refines the σ_multi^(D) usefulness: D primarily distinguishes **orbit-type changes** (different K, different individual symmetry, different swap structure), not within-orbit-type variations.
