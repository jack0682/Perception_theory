## §2. Conceptual Hazards in Consecutive Slack-Days Mode

Three hazards specific to Day-3-after-right-sized-Day-2:

**(H1') Confidence inflation, recursive.** Day 1 over-delivered; Day 2 right-sized at Silver/Gold via cheap proposal-pattern; Day 3 default temptation is "we can do Stage 1 cheaply too" — but the proposal-pattern doesn't apply to Stage 1 (which is per-file canonical-adjacent header drafting, not a one-off proposal). The Silver/Gold success is structurally *not* generalizable to Stage 1. Day 3 needs the same adversarial cold-read discipline as Day 2's G2.1 audit: are we treating Stage 1 as proposal-pattern-cheap when it isn't?

**(H2') Scope creep, doubled.** With Day 2 closing the Silver+Gold path, the visible-cheap-work surface has shrunk to Stage 1 + W7+ NQ candidates (NQ-G1-1-ext / NQ-G1-2-ext / NQ-G3-1-ext / NQ-G1-4 / NQ-G1-6). The temptation to "start one of the cheap-looking W7+ items as a Day 3 pilot" is now stronger because Day 2 demonstrated cheap proposal-pattern execution (NQ-G3-1 was 3-min wall-clock). But Option B explicitly defers all of these. Day 3 must treat **even cheap W7+ pilots as scope creep**.

**(H5') Pacing dissonance compounded.** Day 1 ~10-12h + Day 2 ~4-5h. If Day 3 = ~5h (Day 2 baseline), the W6 cumulative starts approaching ~20h by D3 EOD with D4-D7 still ahead — shadowing W7's nominal hours. If Day 3 = ~2h (deliberate taper), the cumulative ~16-17h by D3 EOD with D4-D7 ahead is healthier. The right pacing is *deliberate taper*, not "Day 2 was right; do Day 2 again."
