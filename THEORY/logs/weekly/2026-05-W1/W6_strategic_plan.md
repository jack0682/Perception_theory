## 4.2 G2 [P0 MUST] NQ-L1M-2 CSEH 2007 Factor-2 Sharpness

### 4.2.1 Goal statement

L-M-2 §5.4에서 사용한 bottleneck-stability factor-2 ($|\ell_i - \ell_i^{(u^{(j)})}| \le 2 \cdot \rho_{\mathrm{pert}}/2 = \rho_{\mathrm{pert}}$)가 sharp인지 점검 + 가능 시 tighter bound 도출.

### 4.2.2 Mathematical depth

**Question**: CSEH 2007 Theorem 4.2 ($W_\infty(\mathrm{Dgm}(f), \mathrm{Dgm}(g)) \le \|f - g\|_\infty$)에서 individual bar의 length 변화는 어떻게 bounded?

**Standard analysis**: bar $[d_i, b_i]$가 perturbation 후 $[d_i', b_i']$로 변경; bottleneck matching이 $|b_i - b_i'| \le \delta$ + $|d_i - d_i'| \le \delta$ where $\delta = W_\infty$. 따라서 $|\ell_i - \ell_i'| = |(b_i - d_i) - (b_i' - d_i')| \le |b_i - b_i'| + |d_i - d_i'| \le 2\delta$.

**Sharpness 점검**: 위의 $|\ell_i - \ell_i'| \le 2\delta$는 birth와 death가 *반대 방향*으로 shift 시 sharp. terminal-death convention 하에서는 $d_i = d_i' = 0$ (terminal-death survivors), 즉 $|d_i - d_i'| = 0$. 이 경우 $|\ell_i - \ell_i'| = |b_i - b_i'| \le \delta$ — **factor 2가 1로 줄어듦**.

**Implication**: Type-N bars under terminal-death convention에서 factor-2 → 1 sharpening 가능. 이로 인해 §5.4의 bound가 $\ell_i \le \ell_{\min} - 3\rho_{\mathrm{pert}} + \rho_{\mathrm{pert}}/2 = \ell_{\min} - 5\rho_{\mathrm{pert}}/2$로 tighten. 따라서 $\tau_* = \min(5\rho_{\mathrm{pert}}/2, \rho_{\mathrm{res}}, r_{\mathrm{birth}})$ — 25% 확장.

### 4.2.3 Subtleties

- **Terminal-death의 정확한 정의**: T-L1-F의 P0 ("terminal-death $H_0$ superlevel persistence convention")가 모든 bars의 death를 0으로 두는지, 또는 일부만 (terminal survivors만) 두는지 정확히 확인.
- **Type-N bars가 terminal인지**: dominant bars가 terminal-death survivors이지만, non-dominant bars (Type-N)는 finite death value를 가질 수 있음. 이 경우 factor-2는 sharp.
- **Mixed case**: Type-N bars 중 일부는 terminal (survivor), 일부는 finite-death (merged). per-bar 분석이 필요할 수 있음.

$$

# §12. Comparative Theoretical Position — SCC vs the Field (Multi-Formation Persistence Edition)

## 12.1 SCC's Multi-Formation Count Theory vs Persistent Homology Standard

**Standard PH (Cohen-Steiner-Edelsbrunner-Harer 2007)**: bottleneck stability of persistence diagrams under $L^\infty$ perturbation. Foundational result.

**SCC's T-L1-F + L1-M**: 
- Uses CSEH 2007 as black-box (L-M-2's bottleneck-stability factor analysis).
- Adds *labeled* bijection $\mathcal A_{\mathrm{bar}}$ between active slots and dominant terminal $H_0$ bars — beyond standard unlabeled PH.
- Conditional on regime $(P0)$–$(P11)$ — explicit hypothesis package, not generic statement.

**Differentiation**: SCC의 *labeled* count + *conditional* regime은 standard PH보다 *strong-er statement under restrictive regime*. Comparable to DTM (Distance-To-Measure) framework's labeled bottleneck variants but with different motivation.

**Position**: SCC does NOT replace standard PH; uses it as foundation + adds multi-formation-specific structure. Per CN10, contrastive not reductive.

## 12.2 SCC's Soft Count vs Standard Topological Soft Counts

**Standard soft counts**:
- Persistence-based soft counts (Cohen-Steiner et al. various) — typically continuous, often $\sum_i \phi(\ell_i)$ for monotone $\phi$.
- Wasserstein-based smooth analogs.
- Topological persistence vector embeddings.

**SCC's L-M $K_{\mathrm{soft}}^\phi$**: 
- Restricts $\phi$ to $\Phi_{\mathrm{res}}(\ell_{\min}, \tau)$ class (not arbitrary monotone Lipschitz).
- F4 (sub-threshold suppression) excludes default $\phi$-sat (empirically failing per WQ-LAT-1.B).
- F5 (dominant retention) ensures count-faithful interpretation.

**Differentiation**: SCC's *restricted envelope class* is empirically motivated (WQ-LAT-1.B) — different from generic mathematical $\phi$ choices in literature. The restriction itself is a SCC-specific contribution.

**Position**: $\Phi_{\mathrm{res}}$ class is candidate for canonical envelope class in multi-formation persistence count, even outside SCC theory.

## 12.3 SCC's Hard-Bar / Active-Count Bridge vs LSW Theory

**Lifshitz-Slyozov-Wagner (LSW)**: late-stage coarsening dynamics with $R(t) \sim t^{1/3}$ scaling for 3D bulk diffusion.

**SCC's W5 LSW analyses (Phase 9-10, NQ-241, NQ-243)**:
- α plateau 0.25-0.30 in 2D SCC.
- $\Delta t \propto t^{1.315}$ K-jump scaling LSW-consistent.
- 3D NQ-244 (W5 Day 6 launched, W6 D4 analysis) target.

**Differentiation**: SCC LSW analyses are *empirical* (Cat A/B for plateau; Cat C for theoretical derivation). Standard LSW is rigorous mean-field theory; SCC's LSW connection is via T4 SCC↔CH correspondence (Phase 8 Cat B target).

**Position**: SCC theory + LSW connection is *cross-correspondence not reduction* (CN10). T-L1-F itself is independent of LSW (counts states under regime $(P0)$–$(P11)$, not asymptotics).

## 12.4 SCC's σ-framework vs Standard Spectral Geometry

**Standard spectral geometry**: Hessian eigenvalues + irrep classification of critical points (Morse theory, equivariant Morse).

**SCC's σ-framework (CV-1.5.2)**: 
- σ(u*) = (𝓕; {(n_k, [ρ_k], λ_k)}) — combines $\mathcal F$ (peak count) + nodal count + irrep + eigenvalue.
